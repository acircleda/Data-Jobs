Director of Institutional Effectiveness

Institution:
Casper College
Location:
Casper, WY
Category:
Admin - Assessment, Accreditation, and Compliance
Posted:
01/14/2020
Application Due:
02/07/2020
Type:
Full-Time
Salary:
$80,000 to $85,000 USD Per Year
Summary:
The position of Director of Institutional Effectiveness provides strategic leadership for institutional assessment and research to support the College’s mission and decision-making processes. The Director also supports ongoing quality improvement of instruction through institutional student learning outcomes assessment. This position reports to the Vice President of Academic Affairs.

Responsibilities:
The Director of Institutional Effectiveness provides strategic leadership in the overall continuous improvement of the College. The director has responsibility to conduct campus-wide research and assessment including the organization, analysis, interpretation, and dissemination of data as well as facilitating assessment processes across campus to improve instruction and student experience. At the direction of the Vice President for Academic Affairs, the Director of Institutional Effectiveness leads strategic data use planning, project coordination, and policy development in support of campus needs. The director is responsible for creating an assessment plan that produces clear evidence of student learning in the institution, and is also responsible for the preparation and oversight of accreditation reports to ensure compliance with regional, state, and federal requirements.

Essential Duties:
Provide leadership and oversight for the college’s ongoing, comprehensive, integration, and research-based planning and evaluation process
Provide leadership and supervision for the Department of Institutional Effectiveness including a Research Scientist, Institutional Researcher, and administrative assistant
Provide leadership and assistance to college academic and administrative departments for planning and measuring program/course/institutional student learning outcomes
Responsible for monitoring institutional effectiveness and improvement including regional and professional accreditation, program reviews, learning outcomes assessments, and course evaluations
Oversee the annual development and distribution of program data to faculty, staff, and administrators to address enrollment and retention of students and draw conclusions and/or make recommendations for process areas of improvement.
Provide support for the on-going development and implementation of college-wide program review, course evaluations, and other support software systems
Develop, assess, and maintain processes and procedures that focus on continuous improvement of the quality and effectiveness of the College in relation to the College mission
Provide professional development for faculty and staff, including workshops and other instructional experiences that contribute to a culture of continuous improvement, analysis of data, and reporting methods and results to internal and external stakeholders
Assist executive level leaders in creating a culture around data driven decision-making
Lead campus-wide data governance efforts and serve as point of contact for the State Data Governance and Institutional Research Council
Facilitate the development of data management policies, procedures, documentation, and implementation of best institutional research practices
Assist in the development of survey instruments as needed
Assist with mandatory compliance reporting, ad hoc reporting, and institutional reporting requirements
Serve as the College’s Chief Reporting Official for coordinating reporting to HLC, IPEDS, and other state and federal agencies
Oversee the creation and maintenance dashboards and other reports reflecting current and historic institutional data
Identify possible data integrity issues and assist to resolve these issues
Support special campus projects by providing access to reliable quality information
Maintain current knowledge of assessment programs, activities, and legislation relating to institutional assessment and research, reporting, and regional accreditation
Develop and manage budgets in respective area
Other duties as assigned

Work Environment:
Typical work environment is an office setting requiring normal safety precautions. Short periods of time will be spent outdoors traveling between campus buildings in various weather conditions. Noise level is usually minimal. Work is usually performed during the normal workday however, early morning; late evening and weekend work may be required during periods of critical attendance to meet the peak periods related to position responsibilities.

Physical Demands:
Employee is regularly required to sit and stand for long periods of time, speak, hear, use hands and fingers to handle materials and use computer, feel, and reach with hands and arms. Occasional lifting of up to 10 pounds may be required. Less frequent lifting of up to 25 pounds may be required (office supplies, books, paper boxes, etc.). Visual requirements require close-up, distance, and ability to focus.

Minimum Qualifications:
Master’s degree in educational research, statistics, psychology, sociology, business, math/science, or related field
Three years of directly related experience in higher education research, data analysis, planning, and familiarity with institutional effectiveness initiatives
Experience with assessment of course, program, and institutional student learning outcomes
Ability to organize and manage workflow of simultaneous tasks and projects and meet deadlines
Ability to work collaboratively with college employees and partners
Exceptional interpersonal, verbal and written communication skills
Experience with data mining and predictive analytics
Experience with data analysis tools and reporting software
Knowledge of applicable data privacy practices and laws
Proven project management skills
Thorough understanding of academic programs
Understanding of database design

Desired Qualifications:
Doctorate in educational research, statistics, psychology, sociology, business, math/science, or related field
Five years experience in higher education research
Experience in supervision of staff
Experience in survey design and administration
Experience creating interactive data visualization tools
Mastery of the Colleague student information system

To Apply:
Completed Casper College online application
Resume
Cover Letter
Copies of Transcripts (official transcripts required upon employment)

Note:
Employment contingent upon successful background check
This is a full-time, benefited position.
