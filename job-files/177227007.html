University of Alaska Anchorage Description:
Serving almost 18,000 students, the University of Alaska Anchorage (UAA) is the largest of the three universities in the University of Alaska system. Recognized by the Carnegie Foundation as community-engaged, UAA is a comprehensive university consisting of a main campus in Anchorage, four community campuses in southcentral Alaska, and several extension sites in rural communities and on military installations. UAA has a unique university mission, spanning occupational certificates and AA degrees to practice doctorates and PhD degrees. The university offers graduate degrees in conjunction with partner organization and offer the Medical Degree through collaborative arrangements with the University of Washington Seattle.

The UAA community celebrates a rich cultural heritage and diverse student body with Alaska's indigenous peoples accounting for the largest percentage of students of color. Many of our students are 1st generation college students. The attractive wooded campus at the foot of the Chugach Mountains serves as a central hub for Alaska's largest city. Anchorage has urban amenities, exceptional outdoor recreation opportunities, and is home to peoples of many backgrounds, with more than 90 languages spoken in the homes of public school students. Our graduates serve the breadth of Alaskan communities, from very small, very remote Alaska Native villages, to medium-size fishing communities, to metropolitan Anchorage.

Purpose of the Position:
Alaska experiences elevated social indicators suggesting considerable need to expand and deepen its capacities for addressing substance use dependency and abuse. This position will increase the capacity of the UAA Department of Human Services to educate students in human services to advance substance use treatment and intervention in the State of Alaska. In partnership with the faculty of the Department of Human Services and other academic units of the University of Alaska Anchorage College of Health, the incumbent will work closely with academic and practice partners for strengthening and expanding inter-professional collaboration in substance use treatment education. The incumbent will also work closely with state, nonprofit, and tribal providers of substance use treatment, advancing programs of innovation and facilitating sustainability of intervention programs and efforts. The incumbent will work closely with lead human service faculty to develop the Substance Use Intervention and Treatment Education (SUITE) office that will be based in the UAA Department of Human Services.

Supervision and Workload:
The position will serve as a faculty member of the UAA Department of Human Services and will be responsible to the Chair of the department and to the Director of the UAA School of Social Work. The incumbent will have an active workload emphasizing teaching and instruction, and other instructional activities, and it will focus service on the advancement of substance use treatment education and substance use treatment programs within community and provider organizations, including public, nonprofit, and tribal entities.

Core Responsibilities:

  1. Build strong and positive working relationships with staff members in state, nonprofit, and tribal substance use treatment programs, and come to understand their workforce development needs
  2. In partnership with the principals of the UAA Department of Human Services and community partners, advance the currency of educational competencies guiding student development in substance use treatment practice
  3. Teach content, modules, and courses in human services and substance use treatment
  4. In partnership with state, nonprofit, and tribal organizations, create relevant and high quality practicum experiences for students in substance use treatment, rehabilitation, and recovery
  5. Provide technical assistance to community partners seeking to advance innovation in substance use treatment and substance use treatment education on a pre-service and continuing educational basis
  6. Participate in the preparation of contracts and proposals for external funding to advance the innovation and sustainability of substance use treatment education
  7. Advance the cultural responsiveness of substance use treatment and substance use treatment education within community settings
  8. Build internal awareness of the Department of Human Service faculty and the faculty of other academic partners to better understand the workforce needs of employers of child welfare personnel

Knowledge/Skills/Abilities:

  • Strong inter-professional and communication skills, particularly within group situations
  • Demonstrated ability to communicate effectively as an instructor within diverse educational forums including both face-to-face instruction and distance delivered instruction
  • Conversant in use of technologies for distance delivery of educational content
  • Strong knowledge base of substance use treatment in human services practice education
  • Knowledge and skills in substance use treatment field education within undergraduate and graduate human service programs
  • Documented evidence of building and sustaining strong community-university partnerships, particularly in the area of substance use treatment education
  • Commitment to and the ability to work effectively within the scope of the College of Health mission
  • Skill in educating human service students about equity and inequity across the life course
  • Strong written and oral presentation skills
  • Strong computer literacy including software and tools for assessment and evaluation
  • Knowledge of human service practice in substance use education and its transmission within educational contexts
  • Ability to work effectively in faculty teams
  • Commitment to ongoing professional development
  • Capacity to work with community partners in the integration of substance use treatment in tribal, state government, and/or nonprofit settings

Required Experience:

  • At least three years of post-degree practice experience
  • Experience in working with diverse groups and populations in substance use treatment and in substance use treatment education
  • Knowledge of substance use trends and issues within Alaska or comparable states, and/or nationally

Preferred:

  • Master's or Ph.D.
  • Licensure or license eligibility in social work, counseling, or psychology

Application Procedure:
To be considered for the position, applicants must upload the following documents to their online application:

  • Cover letter (please note Multicultural Pre-Professoriate Fellow position on your cover letter) emphasizing your credentials, experience, and expertise in human service and substance use treatment
  • Curriculum vitae or resume
  • Statement of teaching and instructional philosophy in substance use treatment and substance use treatment education
  • Statement of experience in human services, social services, and substance use treatment
  • Copies of college transcripts verifying terminal degree (official transcripts will be required prior to hiring)

Special conditions of employment:

  • Official transcripts documenting academic credentials required for the position
  • Eligible to work in the United States; no work visas are available for this position
  • Successful background check
