Data Visualization Specialist

University of Wisconsin - Madison

Madison, WI

Admin - Financial Aid

Application Due:
01/19/2020

Full-Time

Work type: Staff-Full Time

Department:ENROLLMENTMGMT/FIN AID

Location: Madison

Categories: Academic Advising, Student Services/Support, Other

Position Summary:

This position is tasked with researching and developing data visualizations that support data-driven decisions within the Office of Student Financial Aid (OSFA), the Division of Enrollment Management (DEM) and within other areas of the University, including schools/colleges/departments. This position is part of the Strategic Initiatives Unit, the OSFA team responsible for Communications, Data, Special Projects, and Administrative duties. The Strategic Initiatives Unit ensures that OSFA is on brand and that office priorities are seamlessly achieved. This position will support the Student Success Through Applied Research (SSTAR) Lab which is an extension of DEM and housed in the OSFA.

The position will assist in creating storyboards for data reports and visualizations that supports the advocacy, protection and fundraising of over $480 million annually in student financial aid with special attention given to interpretation of the data by the desired audience. This will require written narrative explanations of visualizations as well as collaborative approach with multiple units within and outside of OSFA including but not limited to the Office of the Registrar, the Office of Undergraduate Admissions and Recruitment, the Division of Enrollment Management, the Bursar's Office, UW System Administration, Office of Academic Planning and Institutional Research, Office of Budget Planning and Analysis, and UW-Madison schools/colleges/departments. This position will require a great deal of knowledge in data security including awareness and compliance in multiple data security policies, including but not limited to FERPA, HEA, GLBA, UW-Madison, and UW System.

Degree and Area of Specialization:
  • Bachelor's degree required.
Minimum Years and Type of Relevant Work Experience:
  • At least one (1) year in a role demonstrating strong analytic skills with student and/or higher education data.
  • Excellent written and oral communication skills.
  • Strong attention to detail, accuracy and organization.
  • Ability to analyze and connect data, distill data into formats appropriate for dissemination, and to tell a story with data.
  • The ability to provide narrative explanations of visualizations is key.
  • Experience utilizing data visualization tools (e.g. Tableau, D3, HighCharts) is required.
  • Understanding of database management systems and querying methods is also required.
