Data Analyst, Enrollment Analytics

Institution:
The University of Arizona
Location:
Tucson, AZ
Category:
Admin - Admissions and Enrollment
Posted:
01/10/2020
Application Due:
Open Until Filled
Type:
Full-Time
Department: Admissions & New Student Enrol (8102)

Location: Main Campus

Position Summary

The Data Analyst reports to the Manager of Enrollment Analytics in Operations and Systems (OSM) unit in Enrollment Management. OSM houses the following units Undergraduate Admissions Processing Unit (Domestic/International), Enrollment Management and Systems Communication, and Student Affairs Systems Groups. OSM is responsible for the processing and review of undergraduate admissions for domestic and international students, the oversight of the Client Relationship Management system for prospective students, and desktop support, custom program development, web applications and data and reporting. The Data Analyst will analyze the data related to admissions and enrollment to create prediction models related to net tuition revenue and admissions yield.

The Data Analyst will work with the Manager to analyze and interpret enrollment data.

Outstanding UA benefits include health, dental, and vision insurance plans; life insurance and disability programs; paid vacation, sick leave, and holidays; UA/ASU/NAU tuition reduction for the employee and qualified family members; state and optional retirement plans; access to UA recreation and cultural activities; and more!

The University of Arizona has been recognized for our innovative work-life programs. For more information about working at the University of Arizona and relocations services, please click here.

The University of Arizona is in the process of creating a new classification and compensation architecture. This University Career Architecture Project (UCAP) will revolutionize the way we think about professions and manage compensation at the UA. It is a two year initiative that will replace the current classified staff and appointed professional categories of employment with market-based job functions and families. As an applicant, UCAP will create clearer career paths and opportunities and will help you make more informed pay decisions. To learn more about this project, please visit ucap.arizona.edu.

Duties & Responsibilities
  • Analyzes and interprets quantitative and qualitative enrollment data.
  • Conduct statistical modeling and analyses of institutional data in the support of strategic planning initiatives and decision-making
  • Perform data analysis and develop effective models that identify trends and provide information to project net tuition revenue
  • Collect and analyze student enrollment data to develop a better understanding of impacts to yield for student enrollment
  • Conducts complex research and technical studies by collecting, analyzing, and compiling data related but not limited to such matters as enrollment, matriculation, budget and financial aid and institutional planning and effectiveness; prepares reports and recommendations on findings.
  • Collaborate with UAIR (University Analytics and Institutional Research) on data collection and analysis
  • Provide analysis results through reports, oral presentations and executive summaries using the knowledge of data analysis and analytics and enterprise information systems.
  • Other duties as assigned
Knowledge, Skills, & Abilities
  • Ability to work successfully in a team environment.
  • Excellent time management and prioritization skills; ability to manage multiple projects and priorities and meet timelines.
  • Knowledge of research design and methodologies and statistics
  • Knowledge of higher education assessment and program review
  • Knowledge of evaluation design
  • Knowledge of Enrollment Management
  • Skill in data collection, research, analysis, and development of recommendation and/or alternative solutions.
  • Skill in qualitative and quantitative data analysis and interpretation.
  • Skill in effective communication, both verbal and written.
  • Experience with the principles, methods and techniques of developing queries for reporting and analysis.
Minimum Qualifications
  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Management Information Systems, Engineering or any relevant field that provides knowledge in quantitative and qualitative analysis
  • Minimum of two years of related experience
  • Demonstrated experience working with complex data in a relational database environment.
  • Strong interest and aptitude in data analysis
Preferred Qualifications
  • Master's Degree in Computer Science, Management Information Systems, Engineering or any relevant field that provides knowledge in quantitative and qualitative analysis
  • Demonstrated knowledge in statistical modeling and predictive analytics
  • Minimum of five years of related experience in addition to the minimum qualifications
  • Experience using front-end systems including UAccess Student (peoplesoft), and UAccess Learning
  • Extensive and demonstrated knowledge of and experience with UAccess Analytics (OBIEE)
Number of Hours Worked per Week: 40
