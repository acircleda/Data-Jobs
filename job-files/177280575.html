Senior Statistician Associate

Institution:
Tufts Medical Center
Location:
Boston, MA
Category:
  • Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
  • Admin - Laboratory and Research
Posted:
07/07/2020 (Reposted Job: Initially posted on 03/18/2020)
Application Due:
Open Until Filled
Type:
Full-Time
Salary:
76,190 to 118,102 USD Per Year
I. GENERAL SUMMARY:

The Tufts Clinical and Translational Science Institute (Tufts CTSI) is seeking a full-time statistician for a Senior Statistician Associate position in the Biostatistics, Epidemiology and Research Design (BERD) Center. The BERD is a team of epidemiologists, statisticians, and research methodologists that provide assistance and mentoring to clinical researchers in the development of innovative grant proposals and research protocols.

Tufts CTSI (https://www.tuftsctsi.org/) was established in 2008 with a National Institutes of Health (NIH) Clinical and Translational Science Award (CTSA) as part of the now 60-institution National CTSA Collaboration. The mission of the Tufts CTSI is to promote collaborative, cross-disciplinary, full-spectrum translational research that will have a substantial impact on how research is done. Tufts CTSI provides expertise, support, education, and career development aimed at transforming biomedical research across our participating organizations. Among its key resources is the BERD, which provides statistical, study design, editing, and other services to help investigators frame new research projects and submit grant applications. We are seeking a Senior Statistician Associate to work closely with statisticians, epidemiologists, and other staff in providing this key resource to the Tufts CTSI-wide community. Tufts CTSI has a primary mission to support faculty initiation of research grants or collaboration and participation on grants.

Candidates are expected to be experienced in a wide range of biostatistical methodologies that complement the ongoing and developing programs within the BERD. This individual will have opportunities to collaborate with Tufts CTSI investigators in the development of research protocols and grants; to participate in a wide range of multidisciplinary research projects; and to mentor students, residents, and fellows, as appropriate. This position may also involve teaching seminars on study design and data analysis as part of the educational programs offered by Tufts CTSI to the greater research community.

II. PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS:
Experience with a wide range of clinical research designs and statistical methodologies is required, with preference given to applicants with experience working in a consulting capacity. Applicants should have excellent oral and written communication skills, experience in grant preparation, and teaching and mentoring ability. Advanced experience with statistical programming (e.g., in SAS or R) is required. Fluency in regulations for the protection of human subjects, and expertise in the development of surveys and databases is highly desirable.
  • Assists investigators throughout the Tufts CTSI community with study design, statistical, and methodological questions at any stage in their research projects.
  • Works collaboratively with research departments across the Tufts CTSI community and engages in a high level of interdisciplinary activity.
  • Assists with preparation of grants, research protocols, manuscripts, and presentations.
  • Devotes effort to specific research grants as a collaborating statistician-investigator and supervises junior statisticians who work on those projects.
  • Develops and teaches seminars/workshops geared to fellows and faculty throughout the Tufts CTSI community.
  • Continues to develop statistical skills through educational opportunities including seminars, workshops, and current literature.
  • May teach statistics courses within Tufts CTSI’s Clinical and Translational Science (CTS) Graduate Program at the Tufts Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences.
  • Mentors and assists students in the CTS graduate program, fellows, and junior faculty with their research projects, from design to analyses.
III. JOB REQUIREMENTS:

A. JOB KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS:
  • Extensive knowledge of a statistical software package such as SAS or R required.
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills, particularly the ability to communicate technical information to non-technical colleagues.
B. EDUCATION:

Master’s or Ph.D. in statistics or related field

C. EXPERIENCE:

Minimum of 5 years in a consulting/teaching role

An equivalent combination of education and experience which provides proficiency in the areas of responsibility listed above may be substituted for the above education and experience requirements.

IV. WORKING CONDITIONS/PHYSICAL DEMANDS:
  1. Working Environment is a normal office work environment. No patient care area/contact.
  2. No Continuous/frequent/occasional exposure to hazardous chemicals/sick patients/bodily substances, extreme noise, radiation, lasers, electric shock, etc.
  3. Work requires occasional/frequent/continuous typing/computer keying.
This is a grant-funded position through April 2023, with the possibility of renewal.

AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES STATEMENT:

Must be able to perform all essential functions of this position with reasonable accommodation if disabled.
