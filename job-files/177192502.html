Library Assessment Manager

Institution:
Ball State University
Location:
Muncie, IN
Category:
Admin - Libraries
Posted:
01/17/2020
Type:
Full-Time
Additional Information

Ball State University Libraries includes Bracken Library and branch libraries in the Cooper Science Building, Architecture Building and Health Professions Building. The Libraries employs 48 professionals and 68 staff employees. With over 1.2 million volumes and 300 databases, the Libraries provides access to a full spectrum of scholarly resources and also offers vibrant learning space for over 1.3 million annual visitors.

The Libraries is a vibrant center for collaboration and learning and advances a strong commitment to student success, lifetime learning, faculty support, community engagement, and inclusivity and diversity.

MISSION:

Ball State University Libraries supports the university's mission and enduring values by creating transformative experiences for diverse communities of learners through excellent resources, expert research assistance, dedicated study and learning spaces, and innovative services and technologies for knowledge discovery and dissemination, lifetime learning, and community engagement.

VISION:

Ball State University Libraries is an effective leader and essential partner in the success of our students, the advancement of our faculty, and the progress of our community. We innovate to connect people, cultures, and knowledge as a center for lifelong learning and a catalyst for new ideas.

Benefits and Community: https://www.bsu.edu/about/administrativeoffices/humanresources/jobs/benefits-and-community/professional-staff

Relocation
https://www.bsu.edu/about/administrativeoffices/humanresources/jobs/working-at-ball-state/relocation

FLSA: Learned Professional Exemption

Hrs/Week: 40

Length of Assignment: Full-Time At-Will

Position Function

Provide leadership and management in the development and coordination of an inclusive and comprehensive strategic assessment program across all library units with a focus on user experience and measurable student success and learning outcomes; work collaboratively to develop metrics and methods for the assessment of library services, spaces, collections, and technologies; collect, analyze, document, interpret, represent, and report data to support evidence-based decision making, advance strategic objectives, and communicate the value and potential of the University Libraries; promote a culture of assessment through providing training, consultative services, and engaging partners, collaborators, and stakeholders in developing data skills, competencies, and dexterity.

Minimum Education

MLS/MLIS/MIS degree from an ALA-accredited program or other relevant advanced degree with focus in statistics, service design, business analytics, data science, qualitative research or related discipline.

Minimum Experience

Proven experience applying quantitative and qualitative assessment methodologies; strong analytical and innovative problem-solving skills; experience developing and implementing successful assessment programs to achieve measurable outcomes; in depth knowledge of various statistical software such as SPSS, NVivo, Tableau, etc. and data visualization platforms; working knowledge of basic Microsoft Office applications.

Minimum Other

Excellent interpersonal skills, with the ability to establish positive and productive collaborations working with diverse audiences and colleagues throughout the Libraries and beyond; excellent oral and written communication skills, especially when presenting data; ability to work evenings and/or weekends.

Authorization to work in the US
Candidates for searches must have current authorization to be employed in the U.S. without employer sponsorship.

Preferred Education

Additional advanced degrees.

Pre-Employment Screening
Requires successful completion of a background check and education verification.

Position Title(s) Supervised

None.

About Ball State University

Ball State University is located in Muncie, Indiana, on an attractive campus 45 miles northeast of Indianapolis. Approximately 22,000 graduate and undergraduate students enroll in one of 10 academic colleges that offer 190 undergraduate programs. We offer more than 140 master's, doctoral, certificate, and specialist degrees, with many of them ranking among the best in the nation. Ball State aspires to be the model of the most student-centered and community-engaged of the 21st century public research universities, transforming entrepreneurial learners into impactful leaders - committed to improving the quality of life for all.

Duties & Responsibilities
  • Ranking: 1
    Provide leadership and direction in the development of an inclusive assessment program across the University Libraries; work collaboratively with library administration and unit managers to design, plan, collect, and interpret assessment data.
  • Ranking: 2
    Advise on and apply appropriate current research methodologies, evaluative quantitative and qualitative approaches, and applies relevant standards to library assessment, align research strategies to specific research problems and utilizing input, output, outcomes, and impact measures to inform strategic decisions.
  • Ranking: 3
    Design appropriate assessment instruments such as rubrics, surveys, focus groups, and balanced scorecards to collect data; identify and define key metrics and use statistical data analysis or quantitative research methods to help solve problems, measure outcomes and demonstrate the relevance and value of the Libraries to student and faculty success.
  • Ranking: 4
    Use advanced communication and software skills to synthesize, interpret, and represent data through written narratives, reports, data visualization, graphics and presentations to internal and external stakeholders.
  • Ranking: 5
    Support data collection and routine assessment report production and compilation for various organizations such as Association of Research Libraries, Center for Research Libraries, and IPEDS; maintain familiarity with national library assessment initiatives, protocols, and library standards.
  • Ranking: 6
    Serve as liaison to the Office of Institutional Research and Decision Support to correlate data and align library assessment initiatives to institutional goals; maintain a high level of confidentiality and data ethics, respecting the privacy of library users and staff; work with the Institutional Review Board as necessary to protect the dignity and rights of human subjects.
  • Ranking: 7
    Develop a culture of assessment in the University Libraries working across units to provide training, promote assessment literacies and dexterities, and articulate a clear action plan based on a shared vision and strategic priorities aligned to the Libraries goals.
  • Ranking: 8
    Actively participate in and positively contribute to the activities of the Libraries, university, and profession through such opportunities as related professional organizations, presentations, committee service, publication, etc.; keep abreast of professional literature and scholarly developments in areas of specialization and pursue opportunities for relevant professional development.
  • Ranking: 9
    Perform other related duties as assigned.
