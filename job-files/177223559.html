Associate Psychometrician

Institution:
University of Kansas
Location:
Lawrence, KS
Category:
Admin - Assessment, Accreditation, and Compliance
Posted:
02/24/2020
Type:
Full-Time
16630BR

Achievement & Assessment Inst

Position Overview
ATLAS, a center at the University of Kansas, is seeking an Associate Psychometrician with expertise in educational measurement and mathematical statistics to support the research and psychometric activities of the Dynamic Learning Maps (DLM) alternate assessment and other ATLAS projects. ATLAS projects include large-scale, operational assessments delivered to more than 500,000 students across over 19 states and multiple federally funded research projects focused on learning and assessment in a variety of fields. The responsibilities of associate psychometrician will include managing and cleaning large datasets, running descriptive and statistical analyses using a programming software such as R or Python, performing quality assurance checks on analyses and reports, contributing to technical documents, presenting to Technical Advisory Committees or other external audiences, and other duties as assigned.

This is a full-time (40 hours/week), unclassified position at the Lawrence campus, with occasional off-site meetings. Remote work arrangements with regular travel to the Lawrence campus will be considered for well-qualified candidates.

Job Description
 50% - Perform statistical tasks in support of research and psychometric initiatives. Duties include cleaning data, running descriptive statistics and analyses, and ensuring accuracy of operational and research output.

20% - Contribute to technical documentation for ATLAS projects. Documentation includes contributions to technical reports, manuscripts, and other materials.

15% - Contribute to the development of programs and/or software. Developments will include streamlining new and existing workflows and analyses.

10% - Other duties to support the research and psychometric activities of ATLAS projects. This may include literature reviews, data support, and methodological consulting.

5% - Present to various audiences. Presentations may include Technical Advisory Committees and at professional conferences.

Required Qualifications
  1. Master's degree in educational psychology, educational measurement, statistics, or other closely related field.
  2. Experience with one or more statistical programming languages such as R, SAS, or Python.
  3. An understanding of and experience applying diagnostic classification models to real data as evidenced by application materials and interview.
  4. Effective oral and written communication skills for technical audiences, as evidenced in written application materials and interview.
  5. Detail oriented with strong organizational skills, as evidenced by application materials.
Preferred Qualifications
  1. One year of experience with large-scale operational assessments.
  2. One year  of experience writing technical reports, including results of quantitative analyses.
  3. One year of experience building R packages with documentation (e.g., vignettes).
  4. Experience communicating quantitative information to non-technical audiences.
  5. Strong problem-solving skills, as evidenced by application materials and interview.
Contact Information to Applicants
Cynthia Perez atlas-admin@ku.edu

Additional Candidate Instruction
A complete application includes online application, vita, cover letter clearly addressing how required and preferred qualifications are met, and contact information for three professional references. Only complete applications will be considered.

Application review begins March 2, 2020 and continues until the position is filled. To ensure first consideration, please apply before the application review date.
