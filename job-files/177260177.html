Research Analyst (Assessment)

Institution:
Prince George's Community College
Location:
Largo, MD
Category:
  • Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
  • Admin - Assessment, Accreditation, and Compliance
Posted:
05/08/2020
Application Due:
Open Until Filled
Type:
Full-Time
Department: Strategy, Planning and Effectiveness

Hiring Salary Range: $71,897 - $79,087/Annually

Job Description Summary

Under general supervision, the Research Analyst for Assessment is responsible for supporting the assessment operations and federal and state reporting of the Research, Assessment, and Effectiveness office by retrieving data, providing data analyses, designing and implementing research protocols, designing and administering surveys, conducting environmental scanning, and serving on college wide committees. The analyst will use the College's enterprise, business intelligence, and assessment management systems, SPSS, the Microsoft software suite, and other appropriate tools to carry out these functions. The analyst prepares written analytical reports, and collaborates with other s staff in the Research, Assessment, and Effectiveness office as needed to meet project goals and deadlines.

Minimum Qualifications
  1. Bachelor's degree in social science, computer technology, management information systems or a related field. Master's degree or Ph.D. preferred in social science or related field.
  2. Two years of full-time equated experience in data analysis of large data sets, preferably longitudinal data sets.
  3. Two years of experience in higher education preferred.
  4. Experience teaching in higher education preferred.
Criteria

Data Analysis
  1. Analyzes results, including frequencies, cross tabulations, means tables, multivariate analyses, and tests of statistical significance.
  2. Designs and conducts quantitative and qualitative research projects.
  3. Maintains data integrity for ongoing projects.
Data Retrieval, Interpretation, and Display
  1. Retrieve data from the business intelligence and assessment management systems.
  2. Train and assist College faculty and staff on the use of business intelligence and assessment management systems.
  3. Provides consultation grounded in assessment theory/practice to college faculty and staff on interpretation and appropriate usage of data to assess outcomes.
  4. Interprets data for a broad range of stakeholders.
  5. Prepares and writes reports based upon findings from research projects.
  6. Prepares visual displays of data using Excel, Word, PowerPoint and other software as appropriate.
  7. Instructs and informs others in the appropriate assessment strategies and tools grounded in assessment theory/ practice.
  8. Performs merges, modifications, and edits to data files as needed for analysis
Leadership
  1. Coordinates and assists with the design of evaluation projects.
  2. Coordinates with staff working jointly on projects.
  3. Coordinates with other office staff to provide cross-training opportunities.
Other
  1. Performs other duties as assigned.
  2. Responsible for complying with all required College mandated training within the prescribed timelines.
Job Requirements
  1. Must be available to work between the hours of 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Monday - Friday. Work schedule subject to change based on the needs of the department.
  2. Ability to communicate effectively in spoken and written standard English.
  3. As required by the 1986 Immigration Act, be prepared to present acceptable documentation showing your identity and that you are a U.S. citizen or an alien who is authorized to work.
Is Background Check Required?: Yes

Posting Number: PGCC150911/12

Background Check Statement

A background investigation will be conducted prior to offer of employment. A signed authorization for release of personal information will be required if selected for an interview.

Special Instructions to Applicants

First consideration for this position will be given to applicants who apply before April 22, 2020.
