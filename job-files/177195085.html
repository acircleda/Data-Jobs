University Research Analyst I

Institution:
Rice University
Location:
Houston, TX
Category:
Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
Posted:
01/21/2020
Application Due:
02/11/2020
Type:
Full-Time
Department: Office of Institutional Effectiveness

Full Time/Part Time: Full-Time

Requisition Number: S20344

Open Date: 01/21/2020

Close Date: 02/11/2020

Salary Information

Salary Commensurate with Experience and Qualifications

Work Schedule

8:30-5

Benefits Eligible: Yes

Eligible for Overtime: No

Position Summary

The functions of the Office of Institutional Effectiveness (OIE) include reporting sensitive and non-sensitive data to internal constituencies and external agencies and organizations, monitoring and ensuring compliance with accreditation requirements, managing and administering surveys to collect data from various campus members, and conducting analytic work to answer strategic questions to assist management in decision making.

The university research analyst I assists other members of OIE in data collection, data cleaning, data analysis, and report generation. In collaboration with other staff and members of the University, the analyst will create, distribute, and analyze surveys of various populations at the University (e.g., students, faculty, and staff). The analyst will develop data visualizations, dashboards, automated reports, and ad-hoc reports to provide institutional data for internal and external audiences. The analyst will also perform other IR-related duties as assigned.

Over time the expectation is that the incumbent will be able to work more and more independently with little direct assistance and oversight from other team members.

Education Required: Bachelor's Degree

Major/Discipline

Social science, psychology, statistics, business, or other related disciplines involving data analytics and data elements associated with the social sciences, business, or education.

Substitution for Education Requirement
Experience may not be substituted for the education requirement.

Education Preferred:

Master's degree

Experience Required: 1 year

Type of Experience:

In any of the following:

  • Experience with statistical analytic software (e.g., SPSS)
  • Experience with analytics/visualization software (e.g., Tableau)
  • Experience with programming (e.g., Python)
  • Experience with producing written reports with relevant graphics for presenting results of data analysis
  • Experience may be through coursework, internships, summer employment
Substitution for Experience Requirement
May substitute additional related education, above and beyond what is required, on an equivalent year for year basis in lieu of the experience requirement.

Experience Preferred

Related experience in any combination of the following areas:

  • Data analysis and data management
  • Key performance indicator data collection
  • Quantitative research in social sciences disciplines
  • Drafting research reports and executive summaries
  • Experience with SPSS, Tableau, Python, Google Apps (e.g., Docs, Slides), Slack, Wrike, Adobe Cloud apps, Qualtrics, and Microsoft Office or comparable software
  • Management and analysis of very large data sets, use of data warehouse, and participation as a team member for business analytics
Skills Required
  • Basic knowledge and demonstrated experience using and applying analytical software, database management system software, database reporting software, database user interface and query software, and data mining software
  • Ability to collect data using a variety of methods to improve or expand databases
  • Strong listening, verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to manage multiple activities in a deadline-oriented environment; highly organized, flexible and rigorous attention to detail
  • Ability to use logic to calculate data; efficiently construct a database or scrutinize the form of a question
  • Ability to work with data of varying levels of quality and validity
  • Demonstrated ability to produce data in a clear and understandable manner meeting user requirements
  • Ability to work effectively with multiple internal and external customers
Skills Preferred
  • Knowledge of data documentation
  • Good interpersonal skills
  • Familiarity with key performance indicator development methodology
  • Working knowledge of relational databases and experience routinely accessing enterprise data in a data warehouse
  • Ability to clearly document analytical methods and findings and summarize results
  • Strong time management skills and ability to prioritize multiple projects
  • Ability to work both collaboratively with OIE staff and other offices as well as independently
  • Willingness and demonstrated aptitude to quickly learn new skills and software as needed
  • Experience working with sensitive and confidential data
Internal / External Contacts

Internal Contacts: The incumbent interacts with members of the Rice community to acquire data and to consult on research questions and analyses. Members include faculty, staff, and students.

External Contacts: The incumbent has contact with vendors, service providers, and colleagues in similar positions at peer institutions.

Physical Demands

Requires working at a computer for extended periods of time (up to eight hours per day)

Working Conditions
  • Private office environment
  • Monday through Friday, standard 40 hour work week with some flexibility
  • May require extended hours in the evenings and/or weekends to meet deadlines
  • Travel to conferences for networking and training will be expected
Essential Personnel

Position is not considered essential during a campus emergency.

Rice University Standard of Civility

Serves as a representative of the University, displaying courtesy, tact, consideration and discretion in all interactions with other members of the Rice community and with the public.

Security Sensitive:

As defined under the Texas Education Code and Rice Policy No. 425, this position is security sensitive and employment in this position is contingent upon the successful completion of a background check.

Essential Functions
  • Under general guidance, assesses and produces relevant, standard, or custom reports showing business solutions tailored to new trends, demands and concepts
  • Distributes and disseminates reports accordingly
  • Develops and produces dashboards, key performance indicators, trends and other recognized metrics used to monitor and report performance
  • Collects, manages and cleans datasets related to the report generation
  • Researches and reconciles data discrepancies occurring among various information systems and reports
  • Participates in the implementation of data standards and common data elements for data collection and use
  • Collaborates with client / department manager to identify analytical requirements
  • Identifies new sources of data and methods to improve data collection, analysis and reporting
  • Participates in the design, planning and implementation of research projects and data analysis
  • Seeks and builds automated reporting solutions
  • Performs all other duties as assigned
Additional Functions or Information
  • Acquires data
  • Assists team members in the creation of data tools
  • Attends meeting with stakeholders and clients
  • Participates in the design, planning and implementation of research projects and data analysis
  • Identifies, locates and obtains data/information from various sources
  • Designs data structure
  • Builds automated reporting solutions
  • Manipulates and cleans-up data
  • Completes data analysis
  • Ensures information presented is valid and accurate
  • Accesses multiple University data sources: extracts, merges, and/or collects relevant data
  • Uses statistical knowledge to provide support in managing and analyzing data
  • Provides analytic support to administration
