Job Summary

MCC's Office of Institutional Effectiveness seeks a highly qualified, self-motivated, collaborative, kind, analytical, and equity-minded Planning and Research Director. Reporting to the Dean of Institutional Effectiveness, the Director manages research staff and plans, directs and administers institutional effectiveness programs including the planning, research, development and maintenance of complex processes, activities and data analytics in support of institutional effectiveness. The Director serves as a resource for college stakeholders wishing to improve their practice and outcomes through data analytics, research and planning. The Director utilizes current technologies and methods to analyze and visualize data, and ensures institutional data are available to the college through tools such as Tableau. The Director demonstrates a commitment to service excellence, innovation, student success, integrity, and diversity in the performance of job duties.

Essential Functions

40% - Designs, plans, directs, implements and evaluates research activities and business analytics related to institutional research and institutional effectiveness; designs queries to extract data from multiple sources and presents data in visual formats appropriate for the desired audience using tools such as Tableau. 

20% - Assist college staff and leadership in understanding data to improve college procesess and institutional effectinvess; serve on college and district councils and committees as a liason from the IE Office and to provide input on processes and data needs. 

20% - Plans, conducts and reports on research projects and statistical analysis such as market research, student success and retention, equity and diversity outcomes, predictive analytics and other related research topics in higher educaiton aimed at improving instituional effectiveness and student outcomes. 

20% - Supervise assigned staff, particularly in the execution and analysis of research designs and interpretation of results.

Minimum Qualifications

Bachelor's Degree from a regionally accredited institution in statistics, educational studies, business administration or directly related field and six (6) full-time equivalent years of progressively responsible experience administering institutional effectiveness programs and activities, which includes two years of supervisory experience.

OR

Associate's Degree from a regionally accredited institution in statistics, educational studies, business administration or directly related field and eight (8) full-time equivalent years of progressively responsible experience administering institutional effectiveness programs and activities, which includes two years of supervisory experience.

OR

High School Diploma/GED and ten (10) full-time equivalent years of progressively responsible experience administering institutional effectiveness programs and activities, which includes two years of supervisory experience.

Desired Qualifications

A.  Prior work experience in a position equivalent to or higher than a research analyst working in an institutional research or effectiveness office in higher education. 

B. Experience conducting data analysis, research and reporting related to improving equity and diversity outcomes in higher education. 

C. Experience creating complex visualizations and dashboards in Tableau software that meet stakeholder needs. 

D. Experience conducting complex statistical analysis including predictive analytics.  

E. Experience extracting complex datasets from various data sources such as relational databases.

F. A master's degree from a regionally accredited institution in statistics, educational studies, business administration, public administration, mathematics, the social sciences, engineering or other related fields

Special Working Conditions

May be required to work at multiple sites or locations.

Travel to campus during interview/selection process will be at candidate's own expense.

May be required to work evenings and weekends

May be required to sit and use a computer for prolonged period of time.

How to Apply

Applicants must submit a cover letter that details how the applicant meets minimum and desired qualifications.

Applications without a cover letter and do not include detailed employment history information will be incomplete and will not be considered.

Please ensure your resume and cover letter provide the following items:

  • Clearly illustrate how prior experience, knowledge and education meet the minimum and desired qualifications for this position.
  • Provide employment history in a month/year format (e.g., 09/07 to 10/11) including job title, job duties, indicate full or part time experience for each position held and name of employer for each position.
  • Three professional references, preferably current and/or former supervisors. If references are not provided in resume upon application, they will be requested at time of interview.
Posting Close Date

Apply on or before January 15, 2020 to be considered.
