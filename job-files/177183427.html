Assessment Director

Institution:
Saint Louis University
Location:
St. Louis, MO
Category:
Admin - Assessment, Accreditation, and Compliance
Posted:
01/07/2020
Type:
Full-Time

Job Type: Full time

Job Number: 2020-00026

Location: DuBourg Hall

Who is Saint Louis University? Founded in 1818, Saint Louis University is one of the nation's oldest and most prestigious Catholic universities. SLU, which also has a campus in Madrid, Spain, is recognized for world-class academics, life-changing research, compassionate health care, and a strong commitment to faith and service.

All About You

Under general supervision by the Associate Provost responsible for assessment and accreditation, the Director of Assessment supports the work of the Office of the Provost to advance student achievement and institutional effectiveness. 

Job Duties

The Assessment Director's responsibilities typically include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Assist in the development and regular revision of a University-wide educational assessment plan to ensure effective assessment of student learning at the academic program and institutional levels.
  • Directly support faculty/program director/department chair efforts to develop (and revise as needed) program learning outcomes, assessment plans, and assessment reports documenting use of student achievement data informing improvements to curricula and/or pedagogy.
  • Lead the University Assessment Committee, a group of faculty representing each SLU college/school that provides peer feedback on program assessment plans and reports. Monitor and centralize the reporting of assessment of student learning across all colleges/schools at both the graduate and undergraduate levels.
  • Lead efforts to assess student learning in the new University Core Curriculum.
  • Collaborate regularly with colleagues in the Reinert Center to support excellence in pedagogy, curriculum design, and related assessment work.
  • Serve on multiple committees, including: Undergraduate Academic Affairs Committee, Graduate Academic Affairs Committee, University Program Review Committee, Distance Education Committee.
  • Collect and report on assessment data for internal (e.g., academic program review and prioritization) and external (e.g., HLC accreditation, specialized accreditation, federal DOE compliance) purposes.
  • Maintain and update a University-wide website for assessment featuring resources for faculty.
  • Support assessment-related elements of specialized/disciplinary accreditation efforts throughout SLU's colleges and schools.
  • Implement and support assessment and accountability management technologies to facilitate assessment throughout the University, including SLU's course evaluation system.

Knowledge, Skills, Abilities, and Personal Characteristics

  • Knowledge of and experience in employing best practices in assessment of student learning
  • Knowledge of and experience with meeting institution- and program-level accreditation standards.
  • Knowledge of quantitative and qualitative research design sufficient to fulfill job responsibilities.
  • Knowledge of and experience utilizing assessment management and accountability software within a variety of academic disciplines.
  • Knowledge of faculty culture and the range of faculty responses to and experiences with assessment.
  • Superior written, oral, and interpersonal communication skills.

Minimum Qualifications

  • Master's degree required (doctoral degree preferred)
  • Three years of educational assessment-related experience in higher education at both the undergraduate and graduate levels required (five years preferred)
