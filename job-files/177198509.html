Statistician/Data Scientist

Institution:
University of Kansas
Location:
Lawrence, KS
Category:
Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
Posted:
01/24/2020
Type:
Full-Time
16383BR

Analytics & Inst Research

Position Overview

Work Schedule: Monday - Friday, 8am - 5pm

The University of Kansas seeks a Statistician/Data Scientist to provide advanced analytics in a wide range of business lines within the university (e.g. student, academic, financial, human resources, operations, etc.). The position will report to the Chief Data Officer within the Analytics and Institutional Research (AIR) office. The successful incumbent should have a broad and deep statistical background, with a willingness to grow their machine learning skillset through self-study and formal professional development.

Job Description

The Statistician/Data Scientist will design, build, and validate advanced statistical and, eventually, machine learning models to provide key insights to leaders. They will also provide consultative services in machine learning and statistical models to other analysts within AIR and leaders across campus. They will also have strong communication and collaboration skills, especially as it relates to conveying complex statistical models to lay persons. The individual will work with highly confidential data and must adhere to strict confidentiality policies and procedures.

The University of Kansas is especially interested in candidates who have professional experience working with a wide constituency and are conscious of diversity and equity perspectives. Women, minorities, and candidates who will contribute to the climate of diversity in the area are especially encouraged to apply.

KU is a Research 1 institution, the flagship of the Kansas system, and a member of the Association of American Universities (AAU). It is located in Lawrence, a thriving and progressive community, which lies close to Kansas City. The successful candidate must have appropriate authorization to work in the U.S. before employment begins.

Data Analysis & Visualization (60%)
  • Serves as a senior-level individual contributor, providing advanced analytical services.
  • Builds and validates effective statistical and machine-learning models to provide descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analysis.
  • Develops new interactive visualizations to provide insights to leaders through expert mathematical, analytical, and computational techniques.
  • Translates analysis into compelling, data-informed stories that educate and empower the campus community.
  • Creates visualizations, dashboards, presentations, and handouts to present conclusions and results in an intuitive format.
Data Design & Quality Assurance (20%)
  • Evaluates sources of information in order to determine limitations in terms of reliability or usability; evaluates the statistical methods used to obtain data in order to ensure validity, applicability, efficiency, and accuracy.
  • Tests and verifies that modeled data meets quality standards.
  • Coordinates with data architects, developers, and analysts to ensure integration of functionality and toolsets.
  • Identifies approaches to improve accuracy and effectiveness of existing analytical models.
  • Collaborates with experts across campus to get an understanding of underlying business processes, strategies, circumstances, and execution to identify business questions and analytical needs.
Data Leadership & Outreach (15%)
  • Serves as a campus expert on machine learning and statistical techniques relevant to the higher education environment.
  • Leads project teams using agile project management methodology, working with data stewards, data analysts, ETL developers and other SMEs to select analytical methods, design experiments, perform analytics, and interpret/present results.
  • Challenges conventional approaches or uses of data, elevating campus analytical capabilities.
  • Identifies opportunities and leads initiatives to enhance our current analytical capabilities.
Other duties as assigned (5%)

Required Qualifications
  1. Bachelor's Degree in Mathematics, Computer Science, Statistics, or a related field and 5 years of experience with inferential and descriptive statistics or a Master's degree and 3 years experience with statistics. 
  2. Experience with machine learning algorithms and techniques such as decision tree, Bayesian, clustering, deep learning, etc.
  3. Three (3) years of experience utilizing programming languages such as R, SAS, Python, Java, and/or SQL.
  4. Excellent communication skills and attention-to-detail as evidenced by application materials.
Preferred Qualifications
  1. Master's degree or PhD in a related field.
  2. Experience with data visualization toolsets such as Tableau, Oracle BI, D3, etc.
  3. Excellent interpersonal and presentation skills, both written and verbal.
  4. Proven ability to produce results when working independently and on a team.
  5. Ability to communicate findings to technical and non-technical audiences and leadership.
  6. Experience in a higher education environment.
Contact Information to Applicants
Nick Stevens, nickstevens@ku.edu
