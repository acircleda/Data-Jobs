Open Rank- Educational Psychology, Measurement and Evaluation (EPME) (Job Id: 38545)

Institution:
University of Louisville
Location:
Louisville, KY
Category:
Faculty - Education - Educational Psychology
Posted:
12/12/2019
Type:
Full-Time
Job ID: 38545

Location: Educ Counseling Psy

Position Description

Open Rank Position 

College of Education & Human Development 

University of Louisville

The Dean of the College of Education and Human Development at the University of Louisville has established a college-wide strategic position for scholar who has outstanding quantitative knowledge and skills and demonstrated success in teaching advanced quantitative research classes. We invite applications for an open-rank, tenure-track or tenured, faculty position in any field/specialty that is currently offered in the college (e.g., early childhood, special education, exercise science, educational leadership, educational psychology, program evaluation, human resource development, and sport administration). We are interested in an individual who has demonstrated success teaching basic and advanced statistical and methodological courses relevant to educational, counseling, human resource development, sport administration, and human performance settings for the college.  The College is comprised of the Department of Counseling and Human Development; Educational Leadership, Evaluation, and Organizational Development, Health and Sport Sciences; Special Education, Early Childhood and Prevention Science, and Elementary, Middle, Secondary Teacher Education. The position begins August 1, 2020. We welcome applicants with research interests that overlap with or complement the diverse research interests of current faculty, though we are open to all well-qualified candidates. 

Minimum Qualifications

  • An earned doctorate in Education, Psychology, Statistics, Program Evaluation or a closely related field;
  • Ability to teach basic as well as advanced statistical and methodological courses such as structural equation modeling, multilevel modeling, and advanced research design;
  • Demonstrated record of research productivity and teaching commensurate with the position level;
  • Evidence of external funding, or potential for funding, commensurate with the position level;
  • Demonstrated ability to collaboratively engage with colleagues
  • Capacity or experience with curricular and program development
  • Evidence of successful teaching and graduate student advising;
  • Demonstrated appreciation for diverse and inclusive students; 
  • Demonstrated investment in and commitment to training students within a diverse, metropolitan framework 

Preferred Qualifications

  • Research interests that overlap with or complement the interests already present in the department, though all areas are open;
  • Engagement with professional organizations associated with the field; 
  • Successful relationship building and research that responds to community partner needs 

Context

The University of Louisville (U of L) is a premier metropolitan research university, and the College of Education and Human Development falls within the top 10% of all colleges of education in the United States. Ranked 12th nationally for community engagement, U of L has three campuses and approximately 22,000 undergraduate and graduate students. The greater Louisville area is a vibrant metropolitan community of approximately 1.3 million people with great restaurants, outstanding parks, an engaging arts community, a vibrant nightlife, wonderful neighborhoods, the Kentucky Derby, moderate climate, and a cost of living below the national average. Please visit http://www.greaterlouisville.com for more information about Louisville.

The College's strong commitment to social justice is evidenced not only in our scholarship but also in our ongoing community service partnerships (e.g., Jefferson County Public School System, practicum and internship sites, community clinics). There are possibilities for research within the schools and clinics and with various organizations in the community.

Salary: Competitive, based upon qualifications and experience

Appointment: 10-month with possibility of summer teaching

Beginning Date: August 1, 2020

Application Deadline and Procedures: Review of applications will begin on January 15, 2020 and, and applications will be accepted through February 1, 2020. 

Applicants may apply online at www.louisville.edu/jobs. Select posting # 38545 for Open Rank, Quantitative Researcher. You will be asked to submit your curriculum vita and complete a self-ID form on this site. Applicants should also send a formal letter outlining interests and qualifications including fit and teaching interests, names and contact information of three individuals familiar with their work, and up to three publications/preprints. Send these materials to Dr. Jeff Valentine at the email address below.

Jeff Valentine, Ph.D.
