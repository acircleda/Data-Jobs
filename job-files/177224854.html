Center for Teaching and Learning Assessment Lead

Institution:
University of Colorado Boulder
Location:
Boulder, CO
Category:
Admin - Assessment, Accreditation, and Compliance
Posted:
02/24/2020
Application Due:
03/19/2020
Type:
Full-Time
Requisition Number: 24366

Location: Boulder, Colorado

Employment Type: University Staff

Schedule: Full-Time

Posting Close Date: 19-Mar-2020

Date published: 19-Feb-2020

Job Summary

Are you passionate about improving teaching and learning in higher education? The newly established Center for Teaching and Learning (CTL) at CU Boulder encourages applications for an Assessment Lead! Working collaboratively with a growing team, this position supports faculty and staff to develop meaningful assessment processes for teaching and learning, as well as assists in translating assessment findings into improvement actions.

The Assessment Lead reports to the Faculty Director of the CTL and works in close consultation with members of the Office of Data Analytics in support of campus-wide student success goals. In addition, the Lead will collaborate with academic programs and support services across the institution to identify and/or anticipate instructional needs and to coordinate services and resources to address those needs.

Who We Are

The CTL supports innovation and excellence in teaching through its workshops, consultations, short-courses, and related activities. It has identified as one of its major thrusts growing capacity for and discussions of evidence-based practices in teaching and learning with a special focus on diversity and inclusion, as well as developing and delivering inclusive pedagogy programming.

What Your Key Responsibilities Will Be

Instructional Assessment and Consultation
  • Assist faculty and department chairs by consulting on the design and implementation of teaching and learning assessment within individual courses and/or curricula.
  • As appropriate, utilize both quantitative and qualitative assessment methods at the individual class session level, course level, and curricular or programmatic level to suggest what to change or continue within specific contexts.
Programming Development and Delivery
  • Build and implement workshops, tutorials, and consultations.
  • Develop, organize, and refine content resources related to assessment.
  • Design and implement analysis methods for short-term and long-term impact of the CTL's programs and services, including professional development in pedagogical practices and course design.
Collaboration
  • Provide expertise in instructional design and student engagement and success to the CTL team.
  • Advise Center director on trends in higher education pedagogy.
  • Participate as an active team member to implement CTL activities and programs.
  • Remain current with trends in inclusive teaching.
  • Work collaboratively with campus network & resources.
What We Can Offer

Salary Range Starts at $60,000.

Benefits
The University of Colorado offers excellent benefits, including medical, dental, retirement, paid time off, tuition benefit and ECO Pass. The University of Colorado Boulder is one of the largest employers in Boulder County and offers an inspiring higher education environment. Learn more about the University of Colorado Boulder.

Be Statements
Be Collaborative. Be Innovative. Be Boulder.

What We Require
  • Master's Degree or higher.
  • Minimum of two years of teaching experience (post-secondary).
  • Two years' of work experience in assessment, evaluation, educational research or educational measurement.
  • Experience working with persons of diverse backgrounds and identities.
  • Record of successful program research, development, and delivery.
  • Experience in crafting and implementing practices for direct and indirect assessment of learning in higher education.
What You Will Need
  • Proven understanding of current best teaching practices and trends in education.
  • Demonstrated proficiency with qualitative and quantitative research methods and design.
  • Proven instructional development ability (e.g., program/course design).
  • Demonstrated understanding of and expertise with principles and practices of inclusive teaching.
  • Excellent communication skills, including writing, presenting, and one-to-one interactions.
  • Familiarity with current literature on evidence-based pedagogy.
  • Ability to work optimally in settings of social and intellectual diversity.
  • Expertise in individual and group training and mentoring.
  • Ability both to work independently and to interact effectively with colleagues, faculty, staff, and students.
What We Would Like You To Have
  • Terminal Degree in education and/or a related discipline.
  • Evidence of professional development (publications, presentations, certifications, etc.).
  • Ability to lead collaborative teams.
  • Experience with digital and online environments.
  • Efficiency in word processing, spreadsheets and database management using Microsoft Office, statistical programs such as SPSS or R, Excel and on-line survey tools such as Qualtrics.
  • Proficiency with data visualization tools, such as Tableau.
Special Instructions

To Apply, Please Submit the Following Materials:
  1. A current resume.
  2. A cover letter that specifically addresses how your background and experience align with the requirements, qualifications and responsibilities of the position.
  3. A short essay (no more than 1 page) that addresses the following statement: "Our unit recognizes that our mission is strengthened by contributions from diverse perspectives. Describe what your commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion looks like in practice."
  4. OPTIONAL MATERIAL: We welcome you to upload a PDF sample(s) of any supplemental material that demonstrates your work in this field.
You will not be asked to upload references at this time.

Please apply by March 19th 2020 for consideration.

Note: Application materials will not be accepted via email. For consideration, applications must be submitted through CU Boulder Jobs.
