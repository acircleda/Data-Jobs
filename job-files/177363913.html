Systems of Care (SOC) Evaluation Specialist

School of Social Work

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

Located in Springfield or Champaign, Illinois

University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign is seeking dynamic professionals to join a partnership between public university social work programs and the Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services (HFS).

The Systems of Care Evaluation Specialist works with the Data Management Coordinator, Office of Medicaid Innovation, and others when directed, to design evaluation processes in support of Illinois Systems of Care and High-fidelity Wraparound programs deployed within Integrated Health Homes.

The University of Illinois is an Equal Opportunity, Affirmative Action employer that recruits and hires qualified candidates without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, national origin, disability or veteran status. For more information, visit http://go.illinois.edu/EEO.

Major Duties and Responsibilities:

Implements Wraparound Evaluation

  • Works as part of a data team to recommend and implement effective data processes to assess fidelity to identified standards. 
  • Works with the data team to implement a database strategy for the project. 
  • Manages data gathering, data uploads, and manual input procedures.
  • Builds recurring and ad hoc data and reporting processes.
  • Performs statistical analysis on quantitative data.
  • Develops and submits written reports as needed. 

Other duties as assigned

  • Assists in special assignments and operational needs as directed by Supervisor
  • Statewide travel may be required

Required Qualifications:

  • Master's degree in Public Health, Health, Social Sciences, Education, Evaluation, or Data Science required from an accredited college or university.
  • 1-3 years of work experience in social science research or program evaluation required.
  • A minimum of one year of experience designing research or evaluation studies and collecting/analyzing quantitative data required.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Ph.D. strongly preferred in Public Health, Health, Social Sciences, Education, Evaluation or Data Science from an accredited college or university
  • Experience in statistical analyses and modeling, experience, and comfort with Stata for statistical analysis is preferred.

Knowledge Requirements:

Qualified applicants must have advanced skills in Microsoft Office applications: Word, PowerPoint, Outlook, Excel, and Access. Exceptional written, oral and interpersonal skills are required, as is the ability to effectively interface with external clients, management, and staff. The applicant needs a proven track record of communicating study results simply and effectively through technical reports, white papers, presentations, and/or publications. The ability to adhere to tight deadlines and work on multiple concurrent projects is critical.

This is a full-time (100%), twelve month academic professional position located in either Champaign or Springfield, Illinois. The position is grant-funded and renewal is contingent upon availability of funds. Salary is competitive and commensurate with qualifications. The proposed start date is as soon as possible after the closing date.

To ensure full consideration, complete applications must be submitted by December 18, 2020. Please create your candidate profile at http://jobs.illinois.edu and upload a cover letter, resume, and the name, address, and phone numbers of three professional references. Interviews may be conducted before the closing date; however, no hiring decision will be made until after the search has closed.

For further information about this specific position, contact sw-hr@illinois.edu. You may also visit http://www.socialwork.illinois.edu for additional information. For questions about the application process, please contact 217-333-2137.

The University of Illinois conducts criminal background checks on all job candidates upon acceptance of a contingent offer. The University of Illinois System requires candidates selected for hire to disclose any documented finding of sexual misconduct or sexual harassment and to authorize inquiries to current and former employers regarding findings of sexual misconduct or sexual harassment. For more information, visit Policy on Consideration of Sexual Misconduct in Prior Employment. As a qualifying federal contractor, the University of Illinois System uses E-Verify to verify employment eligibility.
