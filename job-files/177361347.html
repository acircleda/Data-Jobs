Professor (Open Rank) Educational Psychology, Measurement and Evaluation (EPME); Statistics (Job Id: 40152)

Institution:
University of Louisville
Location:
Louisville, KY
Category:
  • Faculty - Education - Educational Psychology
  • Faculty - Liberal Arts - Psychology
Posted:
11/23/2020
Type:
Full-Time
Job ID: 40152

Location: Educ Counseling Psy

Position Description

The Dean of the College of Education and Human Development at the University of Louisville has established a college-wide strategic position for scholar who has outstanding quantitative knowledge and skills and demonstrated success in teaching advanced quantitative research classes. We invite applications for an open-rank, tenure-track or tenured, faculty position in any field/specialty that is currently offered in the college. The College is comprised of the Department of Counseling and Human Development; Educational Leadership, Evaluation, and Organizational Development; Health and Sport Sciences; Special Education, Early Childhood and Prevention Science; and Elementary, Middle, Secondary Teacher Education. We are interested in an individual who has demonstrated success teaching basic and advanced statistical and methodological courses relevant to the program areas in the College. Interested and qualified individuals at the associate professor level and above will be considered for the position of Chair in the Department of Counseling and Human Development if desired. The position begins August 1, 2021. We welcome applicants with research interests that overlap with or complement the diverse research interests of current faculty, though we are open to all well-qualified candidates.

Minimum Qualifications

  • An earned doctorate in Psychology or a closely related field;
  • Demonstrated record of research productivity and teaching commensurate with the position level;
  • Evidence of external funding, or potential for funding, commensurate with the position level;
  • Demonstrated investment in and commitment to training students within a diverse, metropolitan framework.

Preferred qualifications

  • Research interests that overlap with or complement the interests already present in the department;
  • Experience in teaching at the university level and supervision of graduate students;
  • Engagement in a professional organization (e.g., APA) associated with the field

Context

The College of Education and Human Development falls within the top 10% of all U.S. Colleges of Education. The University of Louisville (U of L) is a premier metropolitan research university. Ranked 12th nationally for community engagement, U of L has three campuses and approximately 22,000 undergraduate and graduate students. The greater Louisville area is a vibrant metropolitan community of approximately 1.3 million people with great restaurants, outstanding parks, an engaging arts community, a vibrant nightlife, wonderful neighborhoods, the Kentucky Derby, moderate climate, and a cost of living below the national average. Please visit http://www.greaterlouisville.com for more information about Louisville.

The department includes Ph.D. programs in college student personnel, counselor education and supervision, counseling psychology, and educational psychology, measurement and evaluation. Additionally, there are five Masters' programs in art therapy, clinical mental health counseling and school counseling (both CACREP-accredited), college student personnel, and counseling psychology. The department faculty members collaborate often, and there are multiple opportunities to work across disciplines within the college, across campus, and within the community. Additionally, we are developing an undergraduate track in counseling and human relations in association with the Organizational Leadership and Learning major.

The College's strong commitment to social justice is evidenced not only in our scholarship but also our ongoing community service partnerships (e.g., Jefferson County Public School System [JCPS], practicum and internship sites, Youth Detention Services [YDS]). The Department is home to two interdisciplinary, community-based clinics (Cardinal Success Program @ Nia and Cardinal Success Program @ Shawnee) that are both embedded in a historically oppressed community in the heart of Louisville. Further, there are possibilities for research within the clinics and with various organizations in the community.

Salary: Competitive, based upon qualifications and experience

Appointment: 10-month with possibility of summer teaching

Beginning Date: Fall 2021

Application Deadline and Procedures: Applications will be accepted through January 15, 2021, or until a successful candidate is identified.

You will be asked to submit your curriculum vita and complete a self-ID form on this site. Applicants should also send a formal letter outlining interests and qualifications including fit and teaching interests, names and contact information of three individuals familiar with their work, and up to three publications/preprints. Send these materials to Dr. Jeff Valentine at the email address below.
