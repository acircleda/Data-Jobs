Assistant Professor, Educational Foundations

Institution:
Millersville University of Pennsylvania
Location:
Millersville, PA
Category:
Faculty - Education - Edu. Admin and Leadership (study of)
Posted:
11/25/2019
Type:
Full-Time
Notes:
included in Diversity and Inclusion Email

Requisition Number: P02289

Department: Educational Foundations

Job Summary/Basic Function

Highly regarded for its instructional quality, focus on engaged learning (through undergraduate research, internships, study abroad, living-learning communities and civic and community engagement), and strong commitment to diversity, Millersville University of Pennsylvania (MU) is a learner-focused institution located in historic Lancaster County, known for its excellent schools, vibrant arts community, affordability and proximity to Baltimore, Philadelphia and NYC. MU enrolls about 7,500 undergraduate and graduate students. We seek individuals who embrace our public mission and value exploration, professionalism, integrity and compassion. Millersville University is a member of the Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education.

The Department of Educational Foundations at Millersville (PA) University invites applications for a full-time, tenure-track position beginning August 2020. Candidates for this position should have a strong commitment to preparing graduate students and new school leaders in educational leadership and educational research. Doctorate or ABD in Educational Leadership or related field. Must have evidence of completed doctorate by date of appointment OR, by date of eligibility to apply for reappointment to the second year (1/30/20).

We seek a scholar-leader who can work with critically minded colleagues of diverse backgrounds and can contribute to the substantive work of the department and university. This position requires knowledge and experience in the fields of educational leadership and quantitative research.

Primary teaching responsibilities will be based on expertise and will involve graduate courses in the M.A., M.Ed. and Ed.D. programs in Educational Leadership. Instruction will primarily be at the masters and doctoral level; although undergraduate courses may be assigned. Actual assignments will depend upon department needs. All members of the faculty also have the responsibility to accept those reasonable duties assigned to them within their fields of competence. Faculty are expected, per the CBA Agreement Article 4, to engage in professional responsibilities such as curriculum development and update; learning outcomes assessment for courses and programs; and participating in department, college, and university meetings and workshops. Expectation to mentor undergraduate students in research or other high impact practices, as appropriate.

Other responsibilities may include working in a Professional Development School (PDS) environment, preparing undergraduate students for certification, supervising student teachers or interns, student advisement and curriculum development. Teaching responsibilities may include teaching off-campus, in the evenings, on the weekends, and in a variety of online formats. Other responsibilities will include scholarly activities and active membership on various departmental, school, and university committees.

TEACHING AND PROFESSIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES:

Academic year workload-24 credit hours (full-time). No more than three preparations per semester. Hold a minimum of five office hours per week on no fewer than three different days. Prepare course outline and syllabus, develop course and lesson plans, and engage in the selection of textbook and other teaching aids necessary to the instruction of courses. Conduct classroom instruction for one or more courses in the curriculum at the higher education level. Teach pertinent course subject using lectures, demonstrations, class discussion, and other appropriate modes of educational delivery. Lead and monitor classroom instruction that promote educational and learning process keeping with faculty academic freedom and responsibility. Foster student development. Participate in co-curricular activities such as department colloquia and honors seminars. Serve as academic advisor to graduate and undergraduate students and be available to faculty and students for consultation for questions in one's area of expertise.

Candidates must be willing to teach day, evening and/or weekend classes and conduct classes on and off the Millersville campus through multifaceted teaching strategies for face-to-face classroom delivery and/or web-enhanced or web-based formats.

Required Qualifications

  • Doctorate or ABD in Educational Leadership or related field from an accredited university. Must have evidence of completed doctorate by date of appointment OR, by date of eligibility to apply for reappointment to the second year (1/30/20).
  • 3 years K-12 building-level or district-level administrative experience.
  • Superintendent Licensure/LOE or equivalent.
  • Demonstrated expertise and experience in quantitative data analysis.
  • A record of scholarship through peer-reviewed publications and/or conference presentations relevant to the position.
  • Evidence of the application of research principles in PreK-12 educational leadership.
  • Clearly identified interest in the influences of poverty and/or technology on educational achievement.
  • Demonstrated commitment to working with diverse populations in inclusive settings.
  • Evidence of a commitment to diversity, equity and inclusion.
  • Strong communication and writing skills.
  • Successful interview and teaching demonstration and three successful background checks: Criminal Background Check (Act 34); Child Abuse History Clearance (Act 151); and FBI Federal Criminal History-Fingerprinting (Act 114).

Preferred Qualifications

  • Evidence of successful post-secondary teaching related to the position.
  • Teacher certification in one or more areas.
  • Experience in teaching distance or blended courses.
  • Active agent of educational change and innovation.
  • A demonstrated record of peer-reviewed scholarship.
