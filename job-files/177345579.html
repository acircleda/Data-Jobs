Description

University of Colorado | CU Anschutz Medical Campus
School of Medicine, Undergraduate Medical Education
Job Title: Evaluator
Official Job Title: Academic Services Senior Professional
Position # 00002983 - Requisition #19469

* Applications are accepted electronically ONLY at www.cu.edu/cu-careers *

The University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus seeks individuals with demonstrated commitment to creating an inclusive learning and working environment. We value the ability to engage effectively with students, faculty and staff of diverse backgrounds.

Undergraduate Medical Education has an opening for a full-time University Staff (unclassified) Evaluator position.

The University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus is a public education, clinical and research facility serving 4,500 students, and a world-class medical destination at the forefront of life-changing science, medicine, and healthcare. CU Anschutz offers more than 42 highly rated degree programs through 6 schools and colleges, and receives over $500 million in research awards each year. We are the single largest health professions education provider in Colorado, awarding nearly 1,450 degrees annually. Powered by our award-winning faculty, renowned researchers and a reputation for academic excellence, the CU Anschutz Medical Campus drives innovation from the classroom to the laboratory to the delivery of unparalleled patient care. Read CU Anschutz Quick Facts here.

Nature of Work
The School of Medicine's Office of Assessment, Evaluation, and Outcomes (AEO) has an opening for a full-time position: Evaluator. The position will apply both project management and operations-focused processes to Undergraduate Medical Education (UME) evaluation-related activities, including annual reporting and continuous quality improvement. The AEO Office is responsible for the design, implementation, analysis, and dissemination of all course, faculty, and program evaluation for the undergraduate medical program to support curriculum improvement and LCME accreditation. This position will independently manage the distribution, collection, and reporting of various aspects of the evaluation process. This includes managing and implementing timelines and following procedures to ensure the timely distribution of evaluations and evaluation reporting. The successful individual will facilitate communication amongst a diverse group of faculty, staff, and students, with different agendas and objectives, to successfully navigate the evaluation process and complete necessary duties.

This position requires knowledge of program evaluation and a high level of technical skills needed to collect and analyze evaluation data. Additionally, this position will require strong writing skills to summarize data and analyze reports, and strong communication skills to effectively collaborate amongst academic professionals.

Supervision Received
This position will report to the Assistant Director of Evaluation

Supervision Exercised
None

Examples of Work Performed
  • Implement and maintain timelines for releasing, collecting, and managing course and faculty evaluations.
  • Coordinate evaluations for the School of Medicine courses in OASIS, Elentra, and Qualtrics including, but not limited to: Creating and maintaining user profiles, importing and managing evaluator classification statuses, preparing data collection instruments, importing, tracking, and correcting session matches, managing match due dates and overdue evaluations, and responding to student requests for deadline extensions.
  • Take a lead role in implementing a new evaluation software (Elentra).
  • Respond to student, faculty, and staff issues regarding the evaluation process, and assist with troubleshooting issues related to the evaluation software.
  • Serve as primary point of contact for evaluation software users including, but not limited to: Helping manage the Evaluation Team email inbox and responding to requests to produce reports and correct matches.
  • Communicate and resolve and/or escalate evaluation process issues in a timely manner. Communication will occur through regular meetings, informal and formal settings, and using clear, understandable language and candid, open communications.
  • Assist the Evaluation Team with coordinating, scheduling, and note taking for meetings and/or focus groups.
  • Review and interpret open-ended evaluation feedback and determine if there are concerns or potential issues that warrant review by Evaluation Team leadership.
  • Analyze survey data including, but not limited to: Calculating descriptive statistics (e.g., frequencies, percentages, averages) and selecting and creating data displays to match the data type.
  • Summarize and report evaluation data as appropriate for various internal stakeholders, ensuring accurate results and protecting the confidentiality of survey respondents.
  • Develop, format, and distribute Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI) reports for each course. This will require coalescing information from various sources such as coordinators, faculty, and historical documents.
  • Perform other duties and tasks as assigned.
This description is a summary only and is describing the general level of work being performed, it is not intended to be all-inclusive. The duties of this position may change from time to time and/or based on business need. We reserve the right to add or delete duties and responsibilities at the discretion of the supervisor and/or hiring authority.

Qualifications

Minimum Qualifications
  • Bachelor's degree in education or related field
    • A combination of education and related technical/paraprofessional experience may be substituted for the bachelor's degree on a year for year basis
  • Two years of experience performing evaluation or curriculum support or related academic support experience
Preferred Qualifications
  • Master's degree in education or related field
  • Significant (3 or more years) experience with and knowledge of Undergraduate Medical Education (UME) curriculum, faculty, and structure
  • Experience working in an academic medical school environment
  • Program management experience in higher education
  • Experience with Tableau dashboard software
Competencies, Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities
  • Experience using Microsoft Office Suite
  • Familiarity with medical school curriculum
  • Outstanding oral and written communication skills
  • Outstanding organizational skills
  • Ability to prioritize tasks and work on multiple projects concurrently
  • Attention to detail and the ability to ensure accuracy in work
  • Ability to work with minimal supervision and show initiative (self-starter)
  • Ability to multi-task and work cooperatively with others
