Director of Institutional Effectiveness

Institution:
University of the Cumberlands
Location:
Williamsburg, KY
Category:
Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
Posted:
01/06/2020
Type:
Full-Time

Department: Academic Affairs

Location: Williamsburg, Kentucky, United States

Type: Full time

Job Code: Faculty

Description

This position promotes, leads and manages institutional capacity for outcomes assessment efforts at the institutional, department and program level across the University. This position manages the institution's participation in all aspects of the accreditation requirements. This position reports to the Vice President for Academic Affairs/SACSCOC Accreditation Liaison.

The institution is looking for an individual who will partner with senior academic and administrative leaders across the community to ask key questions, frame analyses, and use quantitative and qualitative data to facilitate institutional planning and decision-making processes, with a particular emphasis on the school's mission and strategic plan.

The Director will be responsible for collecting, developing, analyzing, and communicating institutional data and other information to constituents throughout the community to support informed decision-making, problem-solving, and strategic planning. Using this data, the Director will also serve as an internal consultant, partnering with a variety of groups to help realize the strategic plan. In executing on these responsibilities, the Director will have responsibility for two key components (1) analyzing and reporting on progress against the School's strategic plan, which cuts across all mission areas, and (2) driving the School's annual academic planning process with departments and select programs and initiatives.

Job Duties:

  • Promote the mission of University of the Cumberlands to all faculty, staff, students and to the community at large;
  • Complete required institutional, program accreditation and other reports necessary for the advancement of university programs
  • Manage a comprehensive schedule of compliance activities;
  • Develop and maintain a comprehensive knowledge of university policies and procedures to ensure compliance with state authorization and licensure requirements;
  • Provide assessment support to academic programs and administrative units;
  • Participate in the annual review process of outcomes assessment reports and program reviews;
  • Provide SACSCOC accreditation support to the Vice President for Academic Affairs;
  • Provide outcomes assessment consultations and workshops for all programs and units involved in institutional effectiveness.
  • Work collaboratively with committees to set the agendas, develop annual timelines, plan professional development, and coordinate the annual program and unit-level institutional effectiveness process;
  • Promote university-wide use of general education assessment results for action and seek improvement of student learning;
  • Liaise with the QEP Director and assist with assessment of the university's Quality Enhancement Plan. Serve on the QEP Assessment Committee;
  • Conduct meta-analyses on activities and achievement of outcomes in support of institutional goals. Disseminate concise and actionable assessments via regular presentations and reports to departments, units and decision-making groups;
  • Promote effective working relationships among faculty, staff and students;
  • Participate on university committees;
  • Perform other duties as assigned.
Requirements

Required Qualifications: A Master's degree from a regionally accredited institution; 3 years of experience with state authorization, licensure, or regulatory affairs in a higher education setting; experience applying for and obtaining state authorization for a higher education institution; ability to prepare comprehensive reports for multiple constituencies; strong knowledge of institutional accreditation, program approval, and professional licensure processes; experience in higher education assessment and evaluation; strong problem solving and analytical skills.

Preferred Qualifications: Doctorate earned or in progress. College or university teaching experience. Significant understanding of accreditation principles, with an emphasis on SACSCOC. Experience in survey design and methodologies. Proficiency with statistical packages and Microsoft Excel. Demonstrated success working effectively with senior leadership. A background check will be required for the successful applicant.
