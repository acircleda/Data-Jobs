Specialist, Institutional Research Data

Institution:
Tyler Junior College
Location:
Tyler, TX
Category:
Admin - Institutional Research and Planning
Posted:
09/05/2019
Type:
Full-Time
Job Title: Specialist, Institutional Research Data

Application Deadline: Open until filled

Regular or Temporary: Regular

Salary Range: Commensurate with education and experience

Job Description: This is a full-time twelve-month professional staff position reporting to the Director of Institutional Research for the following:

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS and DUTIES

Primary Responsibilities
  •          Assists Director with collection, storage and reporting of institutional data;
  •          Explores data on student course enrollment, participation in college programs, academic performance to help the College improve programs and services;
  •          Extracts, compiles and cleans data to prepare files to meet a variety of mandated and requested College, State and federal reports;
  •          Investigates correlations/relationships within datasets;
  •          Performs descriptive analyses with limited supervision;
  •          Prepares output in the form of tables or charts;
  •          Learns and adapts to common institutional research terminology, definitions and modes of practice.
  •          Restructures data files, performs data cleaning operations, and analyzes data using qualitative data analysis software.
  •          Interprets the results, provides customized research reports, and effectively communicates the results in a variety of formats.
COMPETENCIES

Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities
  •          Demonstrated understanding of community college education.  Three years of community college related employment preferred.
  •          Ability to develop accurate reports, charts and graphs that present information in a user friendly, intuitive format.  Experience with Excel, Access, SPSS, COGNOS or similar program preferred.
  •          Ability to clearly and accurately communicate, orally and in writing, complex and/or technical information to lay audiences.
  •          Ability to effectively present information and respond to inquiries from administrators, faculty and staff.
  •          Ability to write, revise, and utilize programs (TOAD, COGNOS, ACCESS, etc.) to extract and compile data from the College's student information system.   Experience with Banner Student Information System preferred.
  •          Ability to work independently and handle multiple tasks with a strong attention to detail.
  •          Ability and willingness to function as a team member in developing and implementing curriculum, accreditation, research and effectiveness projects.
General Expectations
  •          May have contact - in person, by email, or by phone - with staff, students, and the general public.
  •          Must exhibit a sensitivity to and an understanding of the diverse academic, socio-economic, cultural, and ethnic backgrounds of staff and students and to staff and students with disabilities.
  •          Perform other duties as assigned.
QUALIFICATIONS

Required Education

Bachelor's degree in social or behavioral sciences, education, economics, statistics, or other quantitatively- oriented discipline required. 

Preferred Education

Master's degree in social or behavioral sciences, education, economics, statistics, or other quantitatively- oriented discipline required. 

Required Experience

Research experience, preferably in areas related to education, government, public policy, not-for-profit organizations, applied research, or financial analysis. 

Preferred Experience

Higher Education experience preferred.
